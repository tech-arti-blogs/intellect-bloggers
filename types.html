E-learning constitutes electronic applications and processes. The term E-learning can be represented also as computer-based learning, virtual classrooms, digital learning, web-based learning and so on.

E-learning can be categorized into two types

 Depending on interaction

 Depending on delivery medium

A. Depending on Interaction

Depending on the interactive capabilities E-learning can be categorized as

1) Low – interaction

2) Medium – interaction

3) High – interaction

1) Low interaction

Low – interaction systems are those which provide resources via text or multimedia such as PowerPoint or through video / audio.

2) Medium interaction

Medium – interaction systems interact with the users such as conducting test and collecting feedback and learning through simulators etc...

3) High interaction

High – interaction refers to communication via video conferencing online blogs, virtual classrooms etc… In this, the interactivity is very high than the others.

B. Depending on Delivery Medium

Depending on the way the user chooses to learn, there are many types chooses to learn, there are many types of E-learning as follows:

 Blended Learning-Combination of online and offline modes of learning.

 Synchronous Learning-Self – Paced learning with their own choice of resources to learn.

 Asynchronous Learning-Self – Paced learning with their own choice of resources to learn.

 Web based Learning-Web – based learning purely led by internet resources.

 Computer based Learning-Computer – based learning by resources held in storage devices such as CD – ROMs, USB, etc…

 Self-Study Learning-Self-Study Learning from online website/blogs.

 Self-Study with Subject matter expert Learning-Self-Study with subject matter expert learning only from an instructor who is expert with the concept.

 Instructor lead group Learning-It led group learning process in which students may be from various geographical areas who learns from a single instructor.
